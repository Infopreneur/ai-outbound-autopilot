Prospecting

The "Sniper" Prospecting Plan
Objective: Identify 5–10 high-value prospects per day who have money (running ads) or
momentum (expanding/hiring) and a valid "Deep Dive" hook.
Target Profile: Business Owners with $1M+ revenue (High Ticket services) who are already
spending money on marketing.

Strategy A: The "Ad Spender" Method (High Intent)
Why it works: If they are running Meta/Google ads, they have traffic. If they have traffic, they
have a "bleeding neck" problem: wasted clicks. Your bot stops the bleeding.
1.​ Go to the Meta Ad Library.
2.​ Search Settings:
○​ Ad Category: All Ads
○​ Keywords: {Niche} + {City} (e.g., "Roofing Dallas", "Med Spa Miami",
"Hvac Austin").
3.​ The Filter: Look for companies running active ads (started in the last 30 days).
4.​ The "Deep Dive" Hook:
○​ Click "See Ad Details."
○​ Look at the offer they are running.
○​ Side Note Script: "Side note: I saw you're pushing the 'Free Roof Inspection' offer
on FB right now—the bot is perfect for qualifying those leads instantly."

Strategy B: The "Expansion" Method (The "O2E" Angle)
Why it works: Businesses opening new locations or buying franchises are in "Systems Mode."
They need automation to handle the new volume.
1.​ Google News Search:
○​ Search: "{Niche}" "new location" "{State}"
○​ Search: "{Niche}" "grand opening" "{Year}"
○​ Example: "Med Spa new location Florida"
2.​ The "Deep Dive" Hook:
○​ Find the Press Release or News Article.

○​ Side Note Script: "Side note: Congrats on the new location in {City}! Saw the
announcement in {Publication}."

Strategy C: The "Hiring" Method (The "Growth" Angle)
Why it works: If they are hiring "Sales Reps" or "Appointment Setters," it means they have too
many leads and not enough people to call them. Your bot is the appointment setter.
1.​ LinkedIn / Indeed Search:
○​ Role: "Sales Representative" or "Appointment Setter" or "Receptionist".
○​ Filter: "Date Posted: Past Week".
○​ Company: Filter by your target industry.
2.​ The "Deep Dive" Hook:
○​ Read the job description.
○​ Side Note Script: "Side note: I saw you're hiring for a Sales Rep right now. This
bot actually handles the initial lead qualification automatically so your new rep
only talks to qualified buyers."

Strategy D: The "Multi-Location" Map Search (Volume)
Why it works: Franchise owners often own 3-5 locations. One "Yes" from them equals 5
subscriptions.
1.​ Google Maps:
○​ Search: "{Niche} near {Major City}".
○​ Look for businesses with 100+ reviews (High volume).
2.​ The Website Check:
○​ Go to their site. Scroll to the bottom footer.
○​ Does it say "Serving {City A}, {City B}, {City C}"?
3.​ The "Deep Dive" Hook:
○​ Side Note Script: "Side note: I noticed you are covering both the {City A} and
{City B} service areas—the bot handles the routing for both locations
seamlessly."

Execution Workflow (Daily Routine)
Step 1: The Hunt (30 Mins)
●​ Open Meta Ad Library & LinkedIn.
●​ Find 5 companies fitting the criteria above.
Step 2: The Outreach (10 Mins)
●​ Find the Owner/Founder on LinkedIn or "About Us" page. AND/OR send them an email.
●​ Send the "Private Beta" Template
●​ Insert the specific "Side Note" you found in Step 1.
*Make sure your LinkedIn profile matches your AI agency business.

Outreach

The "Private Beta" Cold Outreach Strategy
Objective: Elicit a simple "Yes/Sure" reply from the prospect.
The "Mechanism": We use our internal 1-Click Builder to scrape their URL and create a "Live
Preview" (Their website + Our Chat Bubble) before we ever message them.
Philosophy: We are not selling; we are offering a "private test drive" of a vehicle we already
built for them.

Phase 1: The "Deep Dive" Research
Before messaging, find one specific detail about them that proves you are human. This is the
"Side Note." Do not be generic.

3 Examples of "Deep Dive" Side Notes:
Example A: The Content/Media Dive Use this if they have a podcast, YouTube
channel, or active blog. "Side note: I listened to your recent podcast on scaling
operations—your point about 'hiring for character' was spot on!
"

🙏

Example B: The Expansion/News Dive Use this if they are opening new locations
or won an award. "Side note: Saw the press release about the new Scottsdale
location opening next month. Congrats on the expansion!
"

🚀

Example C: The Hiring/Growth Dive Use this if they have job posts on
LinkedIn/Indeed. "Side note: I noticed you guys are aggressively hiring sales reps
right now—looks like you’re gearing up for a big Q4!
"

💪

Phase 2: The Universal Template (Soft CTA)
Instructions: Copy and paste. Replace {Variables}. Do not include a link or ask for a phone
number yet. The goal is just to get a reply.

Body / DM: Hey, Mr./Ms. {Last Name}!

qualifying leads} instantly.
Would you be open to seeing it?
Side note: {Insert Deep Dive Insight Here}!

Phase 3: Handling the "Yes" (The Demo Delivery)
Once they reply with "Sure," "Yes," or "Send it over," now you have permission to ask for the
delivery method. Since your system sends the link via SMS for the best mobile experience,
frame it that way.
The "Permission Granted" Script:
"Awesome.
What is the best cell number to text the preview link to?"
*If they object over sending a phone number: “Our demo is tied to our SMS
system, so, in order to try it we need an SMS enabled phone number, maybe you
have a company number that can accept SMS?”

The "System" Workflow (Internal SOP)
This is for the team to understand why we are doing it this way.
1.​ Scrape & Build:
○​ Wait for the "Yes" from the prospect and phone number.
○​ Input prospect URL + info into the 1-Click AI Builder.

○​ System generates the "Preview Page" (Their Site + Chat Bubble) and sends
them the magic link.

2.​ The User Experience:
○​ Prospect clicks link -> Sees their website with our bot -> Clicks "Start Demo"

Closing

The "Full Reveal" (The VSL Handoff)
Objective: Transition the prospect from the "Frontend Experience" (the chat demo) to the
"Backend Value" (the VSL) to automate the sales pitch.
Timing: Send this 15–20 minutes after you see they have interacted with the demo bot.

The Strategy:
The demo shows them "what" it is (a chatbot). The VSL explains "how" it makes them money
(automation, lead capture, ROI).
We position the VSL as the "Advanced Features" walkthrough.

The "VSL Handoff" Script (SMS/DM):
Option A (The "Missing Piece" Angle):
"Hey {First Name}, hope you had fun testing the bot!
What you just saw was about 10% of what the system actually does. The real
magic happens in the backend (automated follow-ups, CRM entry, and booking
logic).
I made a short video breaking down exactly how those backend features work to
print leads: {Link to VSL Page}
Let me know if you can see the video?"
Option B (The "Result" Angle):
"Hey {First Name}, quick question—did the bot feel natural to you?
I ask because most people don't realize the bot is also syncing that conversation to
a CRM in real-time.
I recorded a quick breakdown of how we use this to double lead conversion for
{Niche} businesses without hiring more staff: {Link to VSL Page}
Worth a quick watch if you want to see the full setup."

The Post-VSL Protocol
Objective: Close the deal after the VSL has done the heavy lifting.

Scenario A: They watched the VSL & Booked a Call (The Perfect Flow)
VSL page has a calendar embedded.
●​ Action: Prepare for the call.
●​ Script: None needed. The VSL did its job.

Scenario B: They clicked the link but didn't book (The Nudge)
Send this 24 hours later.
●​ Script:​
"Hey {First Name},​
Did the video about the backend automation make sense?”

Scenario C: They ask "How much is it?" (Buying Signal)
Since they watched the VSL, they are price-anchored now.
●​ Script:​
​
“The setup varies slightly based on your needs. We can do a quick call to find out what
tier fits you best? It’ll take 15 mins. Is tomorrow or {day} better for you?”

Summary of the "Perfect" Workflow:
1.​ Outreach: "I trained a bot on your site." (Deep Dive Hook)
2.​ Reply: "Sure, send it."
3.​ Delivery: "What's your mobile? I'll text the demo."
4.​ Experience: They play with the bot on their phone.
5.​ The Pivot: "That was just 10%. Watch this video to see how it makes money." (VSL
Link).
6.​ The Close: They book a call or you follow up to book it.
